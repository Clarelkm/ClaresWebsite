3D Print Handphone Stand Assignment




Handphone stand model from Autodesk Fushion360


This is my 1st assignment with the 3D printer where I am required to design a personal handphone stand. I took inspiration from one of my favourite animated shows where I spoted a book stand that was designed with a butterfly silhouette. I thought it would be gorgeous to incorporate it as the phone rest for the handphone stand.


     

Figure A                            Figure B                            Figure C

     

Figure D                            Figure E


The handphone stand consist of 2 seperate parts.

  1. Body (Fig. D) : Butterfly silhouette design holding the phone.
  2. Stem (Fig. E) : Structure supporting the weight of the entire design.


As for the 3D modelling, I used Autodesk's Fushion360 to ideate. After messing around with the features, i came up with the overall design. Firstly, I took a sketch of a butterfly image as a canvas to trace out in Fushion360 before extruding it out by about 4mm, giving the overall Body a thick and firm shape. Anything lesser than 4mm will make the design feel too fragile and anything more would be too bulky

Next, I have to assess the angle of tilt for the handphone to rest on the Body as well as the length of the platform that holds the phone. Then I move on to design the Stem which must be rugged and firm to prevent the structure from collapsing with the weight of the phone.

In Fig. A and B, I added fillet and chamfer details to make the design more personalised rather than leaving it with sharp corners. Sharp corners and boxy corners will make my handphone stand look amateurish/boring.

Finally, both the Body and Stem eventually came together with a mounting system. In Fig. C, the mounting was designed in a way that allows it to take the weight of the entire structure.

Final 3D printing in progress... (about 98% completed)


Although the handphone stand was completed, there was some challenges faced along the way. In Fig. F and G, the butterfly feelers on top were too fragile and an attempt to remove the support broke it off the entire body. Also, the hole that was meant to fit in the phone's charging cable was too small and the overall structure was too low/short. These mistakes required time to correct before time is needed for reprinting to take place. Through this, I have learnt that I should do up proper calculations before printing out my designs :)!


      

Figure F                                           Figure G


After making adjustments factoring in new calculations of charger thickness and bend radius etc., the final print was completed within a reasonable print time of 6 hours. The design also followed the specifications stated in the assignment.
